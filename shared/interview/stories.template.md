# Interview Story Bank

> Your rehearsed STAR stories for behavioral interviews. The `interview-prep` skill
> reads this and **reuses** a matching story instead of re-deriving an answer from
> your resume — so the wording you've already practiced is what shows up in prep.
>
> Copy this file to `shared/interview/stories.md` and fill it in. `stories.md` is
> gitignored (it holds your real, personal content); this template stays tracked.
>
> Rules:
> - **Keep every story truthful.** A story here is treated as source-of-truth, like
>   `shared/profile.md` — the skill will not invent beyond it.
> - **Tag each story with `Themes`** so the skill can match a question to a story.
>   Use short tags, e.g. `teamwork`, `conflict`, `failure`, `leadership`,
>   `ambiguity`, `deadline`, `initiative`, `mistake`, `feedback`, `influence`,
>   `learning`, `quality`, `stakeholders`.
> - One `##` block per story. Add as many as you like; richer is better.
> - Delete the example block below before you start.

## <Story title>

- **Themes:** <comma-separated tags from the list above>
- **Situation/Task:** <the context and what you were responsible for>
- **Action:** <what you specifically did — your decisions and steps>
- **Result:** <the outcome, quantified where possible>
- **Ties to:** <which resume bullet / profile fact this story is grounded in>
